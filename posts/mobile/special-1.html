Special 1 - Less of a story, more of a rant:

Multi-faceted

  • Today, I interrupt my usual blogging of my journey in the website building realm and I want to get some load off my chest for a bit.
  • Most of the time it seems to me that I am just an amalgamation of the many personalities that I have met throughout my life. And I display an abridged version of said personalities under different social settings and circumstances. Thus, I usually feel extremely exhausted after communicating with people. I guess I have to make an analogy to that of a CPU accessing its cache and work on it to produce a state that is then displayed onto the monitor.
  • An example of me changing attitudes, communication quirks during different situation would best be described by a social instance where I have to remain calm and composed but not extremely serious with a joking manner. I would imitate how one friend of mine would act, talk and interact in that situation with similar vocal tone and accent. Because, in my subconsciousness, I have registered that particular behavior to be appropriate most of the time.
  • That being said, it makes me feel like I’m not myself 90% of the time when talking to other people. I would dare to say that it is one of the reasons why I am bad at maintaining any relationships whatsoever.
  • I don’t know how it happened nor how it manifested itself in the first place, but I know for sure that it is not recent. I think the first time I ever noticed this sort of imitation was when I spoke to friends who spake with the accent of the Northern region of my birth country. I, who had never spoken with that accent before, managed to pronounce with tonal similarities with ease.
  • Truly, the imitations are spontaneous to a fearsome level and they hinder me from being at ease and ‘cool’ with everyone else.
  • I think my rant here is kind of over, as there’s stuff that I cannot verbalize materialistically so I’ll save it for later times when they pop out of my head like this one. :P

- Nguyen Hoang Anh
