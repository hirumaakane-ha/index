  • You can't really revert used paper to its pristine stage.
  • It still stung. Even after 3 years from the whole ordeal in 2014, the bitter taste was still there. I had barely fooled myself that I completely overcame the brokenness. Yet, the bitterness shalt linger for such a long time that it was so hard for me to recover and trust those around me. I distanced myself.
  • To make matters worse, I thought I could mend things but little did I know that what happened and what I destroyed was absolutely beyond repair. All that left was a fragmentation of the halcyon days.
  • No matter how hard I tried to move on, it seemed back then that the ghost from the past stubbornly followed me. Was it karma for the emotions and feelings that I mercilessly put on the train tracks? Whatever it was, it felt so deserving for me to receive this unheavenly scorn.
  • New school, new faces, old faces. Amongst the swirling whirlpool of emotional uncertainties, I tried to keep my head above the water, gasping for a new breath of air, hoping to catch a nearby ship’s attention that would salvage me to a better horizon.
  • At one time, I was picked up my a ship full of hardworking, nice, generally good population. But that is a story for another day.
  • At the moment, I was still giving it my best to reconnect the torn wires. But it all went south for me as, during one instance, I was walking up the marble stairs, thinking to myself for some reasons and I didn’t greet back when we crossed. So, from that point onwards, the lingering ship had officially sailed to other waters, leaving me behind as a bait for the sharks.
